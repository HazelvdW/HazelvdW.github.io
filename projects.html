Basslines, Bodies & Beehive Haircuts

Qualitative Analysis · Thematic Framework · Co-occurrence Networks · HTML · CC0

From disco floors to deep space, jazz to jellyfish — what do we actually think when we listen to music? This large-scale manual qualitative study analyses 14,099 descriptions of music-influenced mental content (MIMC) across 10 genres, using multiple coding passes, multi-coder reliability checks, thematic analysis, and co-occurrence network analysis. The result is a comprehensive "thoughtscape" typology that maps the full spectrum of listeners' imaginative experiences — the kind of nuanced picture that computational NLP approaches flatten out.

MUSIFEAST-17

A published music stimulus set: 356 instrumental 30-second clips across 17 genres, with normative data from 701 UK & US participants (ages 18–75) covering familiarity, enjoyment, emotional expression, genre recognition, and evoked thought types. Includes a Shiny/Plotly app for interactive exploration.
