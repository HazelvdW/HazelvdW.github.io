Music Psychology / Cognitive Science

Research

My work sits at the crossroads of music psychology, cognitive science, and computational methods. I'm fascinated by the inner worlds that music opens up — the unbidden thoughts, images, and narratives that arise while we listen, and how the framing around a piece shapes what the mind does with the sound.

Dissertation Project

In Progress · PhD

Framed Listening

Durham University · Music Department

Does knowing something about a piece of music change what you think about while it plays? This project investigates whether extramusical context cues — titles, programme notes, backstory — influence the semantic content and similarity of music-evoked thoughts (METs). Using a combination of TF-IDF, Word2Vec, and a dual-model BERT pipeline, I'm building a computational text analysis framework to map the imaginative landscape of listening at both individual and group levels.

MUSIFEAST-17: MUsic Stimuli for Imagination, Familiarity, Emotion, and Aesthetic STudies across 17 genres

van der Walle, H. A., Wu, W., Margulis, E. H. et al. · Behavior Research Methods, 57(7), 1–26

A normed music stimulus set of 356 instrumental 30-second clips across 17 genres, with data from 701 UK & US participants (ages 18–75) covering familiarity, enjoyment, emotional expression, genre recognition, and thought-type ratings. Basslines, Bodies & Beehive Haircuts: A thematic typology of music-influenced mental content

van der Walle, H. A. et al. · 2025

A large-scale mixed-method qualitative study of 14,099 music-influenced mental content (MIMC) descriptions across 10 genres. Using multiple coding passes, multi-coder reliability checks, thematic analysis, and co-occurrence network analysis, we map a comprehensive "thoughtscape" typology — the finer connective tissue between thought types that quantitative and computational approaches routinely miss.

In Progress · PhD Dissertation

Framed Listening: Does extramusical context shape the semantic content of music-evoked thoughts?

van der Walle, H. A. · Durham University

Combines a dual-model BERT pipeline (BERT-base-uncased + BERTweet), Word2Vec, and TF-IDF to test whether context cues — titles, programme notes, backstory — shift the semantic similarity of music-evoked thought (MET) reports. Analysis operates at both document and individual MET levels for direct cross-granularity comparison.

Research Interests

Music-Evoked Imagination

What kinds of mental imagery, narrative, and affect does music trigger — and why does the same piece feel so different to different listeners?

Extramusical Context

Titles, liner notes, composer biographies — the stories around music shape how we hear it. I want to know exactly how, and how much.

Computational Text Analysis

Using NLP methods — BERT, Word2Vec, TF-IDF — to make sense of the rich, messy, wonderfully weird language people use to describe inner musical experiences.
