Music Psychology, Music & Imagination

Research

My work sits at the crossroads of music psychology, cognitive science, and computational methods. I'm fascinated by the inner worlds that music opens up — the unbidden thoughts, images, and narratives that arise while we listen, and how the framing around a piece shapes what the mind does with the sound.

Thesis, PhD Project

In Progress · PhD

Music & Imagination: Using music to probe listeners’ imaginative experiences.

Durham University · Music Department · 2024–present

My PhD explores the interaction of music with imagination — our inner "thoughtscapes", images, stories, memories, and abstract experiences that arise while we listen, and what they reveal about how imagination itself works. My thesis is built from four interconnected empirical chapters, each approaching music-influenced mental content from a different angle: (1) curation of a stimulus set with accompanying normative data; (2) thought-type mapping across varied stimuli; (3) computational analysis of how extramusical context information shapes thoughts; (4) a rigorous, large-scale, mixed-method qualitative study producing a comprehensive typology of music-influenced mental content. Together, these chapters weave a multifaceted exploration of the rich inner thoughtscapes that music can open up, using a blend of quantitative and qualitative methods to capture both the statistical patterns and our human nuances in musically sculpted imaginations.

In Progress Ch. 3

Framed Listening: Does extramusical context shape the semantic content of music-evoked thoughts?

van der Walle, H. A. · Current paper

A computational study testing whether extramusical context cues — titles, programme notes, backstory — shift the semantic similarity of music-evoked thought (MET) reports. Uses a dual-model BERT pipeline (BERT-base-uncased + BERTweet), Word2Vec, and TF-IDF, operating at both document and individual MET levels.

View repository
In Preparation · 2026 Ch. 4

Basslines, Bodies & Beehive Haircuts: A thematic typology of music-influenced mental content.

van der Walle, H. A. · 2026

The largest chapter: a rigorous mixed-method qualitative study of 14,099 music-influenced mental content (MIMC) descriptions across 10 genres. Multiple coding passes, multi-coder reliability checks, thematic analysis, and co-occurrence network analysis produce a comprehensive "thoughtscape" typology — attending to the human nuance that computational approaches flatten out.

View repository
Preprint · 2024

Experiencing liveness from recorded music

Rai, L., van der Walle, H. A., Painting, J., & Orgs, G.

An investigation into what extent listeners perceive and experience a sense of social liveness – the perceived presence of other people – from recorded music by adding audience reactions to music recordings, and what this can reveal about the aesthetic experience of recorded live performance. These findings show how social liveness can mimic actual live events, showcasing the importance of social influences for cultural engagement.

Research Interests

Music-Influenced Mental Content

What kinds of thoughts and imaginings – imagery, narratives, memories, affect – can music shape and inffluence? Is there a pattern in the colourful tapestry of imagination woven alongside music, and what can it teach us about each other and our realtions to music?

Manual & Computational Qualitative Analysis

Using human-driven manual readings coupled with NLP methods to make sense of the rich, messy, wonderfully weird ways people describe their inner musically sculpted thoughtscapes.
