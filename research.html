Music Psychology

Research

I'm a researcher in music psychology with a deep fascination for imagination — specifically, the extraordinary variety of inner worlds and experiences that music colours in each of us. From vivid visual scenes to half-formed feelings to memories you'd forgotten you had, I find endless wonder in the fact that the same piece of music can open up something completely unique in every listener. I'm also a passionate advocate for Open Research: transparent methods, shared data, and science that anyone can scrutinise, build on, or just stumble into with curiosity.

Thesis, PhD Project

In Progress · PhD

Music & Imagination: Using music to probe listeners' imaginative experiences.

Durham University · Music Department · 2024–present

My PhD explores the interaction of music with imagination — our inner "thoughtscapes", images, stories, memories, and abstract experiences that arise while we listen, and what they reveal about how imagination itself works. My thesis is built from four interconnected empirical chapters, each approaching music-influenced mental content from a different angle: (1) curation of a stimulus set with accompanying normative data; (2) thought-type mapping across varied stimuli; (3) computational analysis of how extramusical context information shapes thoughts; (4) a rigorous, large-scale, mixed-method qualitative study producing a comprehensive typology of music-influenced mental content. Together, these chapters weave a multifaceted exploration of the rich inner thoughtscapes that music can open up, using a blend of quantitative and qualitative methods to capture both the statistical patterns and our human nuances in musically sculpted imaginations.

Research Interests

Human Nuance in Data

Numbers can tell you a lot, but they miss the texture. I'm drawn to mixed methods — the messiness of qualitative work alongside the rigour of quantitative analysis — because reality tends to live somewhere in between.

Open & Reproducible Research

Good research should be visible, sharable, and challengeable. I care deeply about open data, transparent methods, and building a culture where science is something we do together — not something we hoard.

Imagination & Listening Minds

Can we explore imagination? Yours? Mine? Music influences diverse inner experiences without dictating its content, making it a powerful lens and a uniquely revealing window into the mind's creative machinery. I'm captivated by where music can guide the mind and by how differently that unfolds for each person.

Genre & Context Framing

From basslines to beehive haircuts, discos to deep space — genre isn't a footnote. Or take a title, a backstory, a listening setting — wee nudges that can reshape an entire listening experience. I'm interested in how the frames we place around music colour the worlds our minds create. I believe research into musical experience should reflect the genuine diversity of how people actually listen.

In Preparation · 2026 Ch. 4

Basslines, Bodies & Beehive Haircuts: A thematic typology of music-influenced mental content.

van der Walle, H. A. · 2026

A rigorous mixed-method qualitative study of 14,099 music-influenced mental content (MIMC) descriptions across 10 genres. Multiple coding passes, multi-coder reliability checks, thematic analysis, and co-occurrence network analysis produce a comprehensive "thoughtscape" typology — attending to the human nuance that computational approaches flatten out.

Preprint · 2024

Experiencing liveness from recorded music

Rai, L., van der Walle, H. A., Painting, J., & Orgs, G.

An investigation into what extent listeners perceive and experience a sense of social liveness — the perceived presence of other people — from recorded music by adding audience reactions to recordings, and what this reveals about the aesthetic experience of recorded live performance. These findings show how social liveness can mimic actual live events, showcasing the importance of social influences for cultural engagement.
